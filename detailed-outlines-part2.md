## Chapter 16: The Stakeout

**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words
**POV:** Multiple perspectives, ensemble
**Setting:** Marina, Cyrus's houseboat (exterior observation)
**Time:** After midnight, night after Bobby's revelations

**Purpose:** Witness Cyrus's ritual directly, confirm his religious delusion and active ideology, escalate group's understanding from suspected killer to zealot performing ceremonies.

**Beats:**
1. Group rents small fishing boat, anchors in dark marina with view of Cyrus's houseboat. Hours of waiting, quiet tension. After midnight, lights come on in Cyrus's boat. He emerges onto deck carrying something—waterlogged doll.
2. Cyrus lights candles arranged on deck railing. Kneels facing the water. Begins speaking in mix of Spanish and English—prayers to "mi amor," promising to serve, to keep waters pure, to send sinners to her.
3. The ritual: "I am your instrument. The disrespectful pollute your domain but I purify it. I send them to you, cleansed." He throws offerings into the water (shells, small objects, more dolls). Chants, sways, totally absorbed. Lasts twenty minutes.
4. Cyrus stops abruptly, tilts head like listening. Nods. "Yes. I understand. I will find another. The gulf will be pure." Smiles beatifically. Extinguishes candles, goes back inside. Boat goes dark.
5. Group sits in stunned horror. This isn't calculated serial killer—it's religious zealotry. Jillian whispers: "He thinks he's doing God's work. Or his wife's work. Same thing to him." They need to get inside that houseboat, see what else he's hiding.

**Dialogue Topics:** Whispered observations during ritual, shock at hearing Cyrus's prayers, discussion of what "find another" means (next victim?), debate about whether ritual is insanity or performance, planning to search houseboat interior.

**Emotional Arc:** Group begins anxious from stakeout tension, becomes horrified witnessing ritual (confirms he's not just killer but believer), ends grimly determined to search houseboat. Witness to ideology makes him more frightening—he won't stop voluntarily.

**Key Details:** Waterlogged dolls (evidence of shrine), Spanish prayers (cultural element), Cyrus's conversation with water (believes it responds), "find another" suggests active hunting, ritual proves ongoing mission not past crimes.
